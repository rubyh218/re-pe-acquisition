# Retail — Underwriting Nuances

## Sub-types (very different fundamentals — pick first)
- **Grocery-anchored** — most defensive; necessity-based traffic
- **Power center** — big-box anchors (Target, Walmart, Home Depot)
- **Strip center / unanchored** — small-shop heavy; higher risk, higher yield
- **Lifestyle / open-air** — experiential, dining-heavy
- **Mall** — Class A institutional only; B/C mostly distressed/redevelopment plays
- **Single-tenant net lease (STNL)** — bond-like; CVS, Walgreens, dollar stores, QSR

## Revenue model
- **Lease-by-lease** — base rent + percentage rent + recoveries
- **Percentage rent** — % of tenant sales above breakpoint (track sales/SF for tenants required to report)
- **Anchor leases** — typically long-term, low rent, with co-tenancy clauses
- **Inline shop leases** — shorter, higher rent/SF, more turnover
- **Recoveries** — varies widely (NNN, modified gross, full-service); model carefully

## Critical lease clauses
- **Co-tenancy** — small-shop tenants get rent reduction or termination right if anchor leaves; can cascade
- **Exclusive use** — limits ability to backfill with similar concept
- **Go-dark rights** — tenant can stop operating but keep paying rent (kills traffic, hurts other tenants)
- **Kick-out clauses** — tenant can terminate if sales below threshold
- **CAM caps** — limits OpEx recovery; landlord eats the difference

## Key operational metrics
- **Sales/SF** — by tenant (where reported); benchmark vs. concept-typical
- **Occupancy cost ratio** — (rent + CAM + RE tax) / sales — sustainable typically <10–15% depending on concept
- **Traffic counts** — placer.ai or similar for visit data

## OpEx specifics
- CAM recoverable but watch caps
- RE tax recoverable
- Insurance recoverable
- Marketing fund (anchored centers)
- Snow removal (northern markets — meaningful)

## Asset-class-specific DD
- Anchor tenant credit + remaining term
- Sales reports from reporting tenants (last 3 yrs trend)
- Co-tenancy clauses inventory + which are currently triggered
- Lease expiry schedule + renewal probability
- Visit data trend (placer.ai)
- Surrounding density / household income trend
- E-commerce penetration risk by tenant category

## Risks to underwrite
- Category obsolescence (electronics → online; bookstores → gone; furniture → mixed)
- Anchor closure cascade through co-tenancy
- Percentage rent decline if tenant sales weakening
- New competitive supply

## Data sources
- CoStar Retail — rent, vacancy, sales transactions
- Placer.ai (paid) — foot traffic, trade area
- Tenant SEC filings (for public tenants — sales trend, store-closure plans)
- ICSC reports
