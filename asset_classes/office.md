# Office — Underwriting Nuances

## Revenue model
- **Lease-by-lease** — each tenant modeled with: rent/SF, lease start/end, escalations (typically 2.5–3%/yr or CPI), free rent, TI/LC
- **WALT** — weighted average lease term; critical metric for risk
- **Rent roll roll-forward** — at lease expiry, model: renewal probability × renewal rent OR re-leasing (downtime + TI/LC + new market rent)
- **Expense recoveries** — modified gross / NNN / full-service gross — model recovery as appropriate
- **Mark-to-market** — many in-place rents are above or below market; model the adjustment at rollover

## OpEx specifics
- Real estate tax (often largest line)
- Insurance
- Utilities (full-service: landlord pays; NNN: tenant pays direct)
- CAM (common area maintenance) — janitorial, security, landscaping, R&M
- Management fee — typically 2–3% of EGI
- Most OpEx recoverable under NNN / modified gross structures

## Capital needs (heavier than multifamily)
- **TI (tenant improvements)** — $50–150/SF for office build-outs, more for first-gen or lab conversion
- **LC (leasing commissions)** — typically 4–6% of total rent for new leases, 2–3% for renewals
- **Re-tenanting downtime** — 6–18 months between tenants
- **Building system CapEx** — HVAC replacement ($15–30/SF), elevator modernization, roof, lobby refresh

## Asset-class-specific DD
- Tenant credit review (10-K for public, D&B for private, payment history)
- Lease abstracts for top tenants (>10% of rent) — review every clause
- Sublease overhang in submarket
- Return-to-office trends — utilization data if available (badge swipes, occupancy sensors)
- Class A vs. Class B/C — fundamentals diverging significantly post-2020
- Conversion potential (office → residential / lab) for value-add plays

## Submarket data points to track
- Direct vacancy AND total vacancy (incl. sublease) — sublease is leading indicator
- Net absorption (negative in many markets 2023–present)
- Asking rent vs. effective rent gap (concession-heavy market = stress signal)
- Construction pipeline (significantly reduced post-2022)
- Major tenant moves (consolidation, downsizing)

## Data sources
- CoStar Office — rent, vacancy, sublease, pipeline
- Tenant SEC filings (for public tenant credit)
- BLS QCEW — office-using employment (information, finance, professional services)
- Local market reports from JLL, CBRE, Cushman (often free, useful for narrative)

## Risk callout
Office has been the most distressed CRE sector since 2022. Conservative underwriting essential: assume longer downtime, higher concessions, lower renewal probability than historical comps suggest.
