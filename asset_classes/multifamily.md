# Multifamily — Underwriting Nuances

## Revenue model
- **Unit-mix-driven** — rent roll grouped by floorplan (e.g., 1BR/1BA, 2BR/2BA)
- **Loss-to-lease** — gap between in-place rent and current market rent at the unit level; captured as units roll
- **Concessions** — typically 0–2 months free; amortize over lease term to get effective rent
- **Other income** — parking, storage, pet fees, application fees, late fees, utility reimbursements (RUBS)
- **Vacancy** — physical (no tenant) + economic (collection loss + concessions)
- **Bad debt** — typically 1–2% of GPR; higher in workforce/B-/C product

## OpEx specifics
- Payroll (on-site management, leasing, maintenance) — typically $800–1,500/unit
- R&M — $500–1,000/unit
- Marketing — $150–400/unit
- RE tax — model re-assessment risk on sale (e.g., CA Prop 13 exemption loss, TX market value reassessment)
- Insurance — has spiked in coastal/wildfire markets; underwrite to current quotes, not historical

## Value-add CapEx
- Interior renovation budget per unit (typical $5–15K) tied to projected rent premium
- Pace of unit turns (typically 25–50% of units in Years 1–2)
- Common area upgrades (lobby, fitness, pool, package room) — fixed budget, immediate
- Premium realization curve — full premium achieved by Year 3 typically

## Asset-class-specific DD
- Unit-level inspection (sample 10–20% across floor plans)
- Outstanding work orders summary
- HOA / condo conversion risk
- Rent control / stabilization (NYC, SF, LA, NJ, OR, MN, etc.) — significantly limits rent growth
- Section 8 / LIHTC overlay
- HVAC age + replacement schedule
- Roof age, foundation condition

## Submarket data points to track
- Rent growth (Y/Y, T-3M annualized)
- Vacancy + 12-month forward absorption
- Supply pipeline as % of inventory (under construction + planned-permitted)
- Concession environment (% of properties offering, average months free)
- Median household income → rent-to-income ratio (sustainable max ~30%)

## STR / data sources
- CoStar Multifamily — rent comps, vacancy, supply
- RealPage / Yardi Matrix (alternative)
- Census ACS — household income, renter share, household formation
